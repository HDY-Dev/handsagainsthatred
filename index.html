The Hurt of Many

Starting as a reaction piece fuelled by the hurt of many, Hands Against Hatred followed news of an unjust attack that occured in Birmingham’s gay district in October 2021. A young man obtained horrific head injuries after the brutal attack. The motive? He was holding hands with his partner.

The Bigger Picture

The why behind the campaign is simple. HDY wanted to stand proud in support of the LGBTQIA+ community. Inspiring others to speak up, uniting communities, and spreading awareness of the fact that homophobic attacks still occur. Despite how far we’ve come homophobic hate crimes have risen by 210% in six years. We believe in standing up for what’s right, and so we created a campaign that would speak out for those who have been targeted.

Recognising the power of our voice, HDY are heavily involved with our local community. Earlier this year, we sponsored the Proud Festival, and became the official (pro bono) content agency for PrideHouse, a safe space for LGBTQIA+ spectators and athletes during the Commonwealth Games 2022.
In the early hours of Sunday 10th October, a young man obtained horrific injuries to his head after being brutally attacked with a bottle and hit with an iron bar for holding hands with his partner in Birmingham’s gay district.
Utilising their contacts HDY used their platforms to secure not only free out-of-home advertising, but secured the editor of the Birmingham Post & Mail in joining them. After securing the exposure, HDY set about securing an impactful campaign.

a powerful organic and digital campaign launched.

our fantastic creative team visualised unity and thought by using HDY's hero colour.

our co-founder Ed James took to the streets to produce unifying images in support of the campaign.

The campaign began a ricocheting domino effect across Birmingham; companies, agencies, charities, individuals, and news reporting firms used our approach as a springboard to start conversations.

Our Outcome

The spark that provoked a social phenomenon. Hands Against Hatred commanded attention from millions of social media scrollers and high street bypassers all through a strong visual that was centred around one simple act. Holding hands. The photo circulated the net and #HandsAgainstHatred saw a reach of 24 million, becoming the talk of the city collecting 994 mentions, 167 thousand shares and 4.2 thousand likes.

2022 European Content Awards Finalists

The European Content Awards celebrates high quality, valuable content that attracts, informs, engages and retains an audience. After standing out of the crowd and standing up for a cause, HDY’s Hands Against Hatred Campaign made it to the awards shortlist in the category of Not For Profit / Charity Content Campaign of the Year.
